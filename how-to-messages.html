Messages How-To Guide

The Messages area in CORE by fibodo lets you communicate directly with clients or groups, all in one place. Use it for quick conversations, updates, and ongoing engagement without switching tools.

1. Open Messages

Where to go:
Use the left-hand menu and click Messages.

What you’ll see:

  • A list of recent conversations on the left
  • An empty conversation panel until a chat is selected
  • A search bar to find clients
  • A New Group button

2. View and reply to an existing conversation

To open a conversation:

  1. Click a client name from the chat list.
  2. The full conversation will appear on the right.
  3. Read incoming messages and type your reply.
  4. Send your message to continue the conversation.

Tip: Unread messages are highlighted so you can respond quickly.

3. Search for a client conversation

If you have many conversations, use the search bar.

  1. Click into the Search clients… field.
  2. Type the client’s name.
  3. Select the conversation to open it.

4. Create a new group message

You can message multiple clients at once by creating a group.

  1. Click New Group.
  2. Enter a Group Name.
  3. Add an optional description (up to 200 characters).
  4. Click Next to continue.

5. Add participants to a group

After creating the group details:

  1. Select the clients you want to invite to the group.
  2. Confirm your selection.
  3. The group conversation is created and ready to use.

Use groups for:

  • Classes or group sessions
  • Announcements or updates
  • Ongoing programme communication

6. Switch between individual and group chats

Use the Groups toggle in the chat panel to switch views.

  • Turn Groups off to see individual messages
  • Turn Groups on to see group conversations

7. Messaging best practice

  • Use messages for quick, informal communication
  • Keep important instructions clear and concise
  • Use group messages to reduce repeated admin
  • Respond promptly to unread messages to improve client experience

Result:
Messages keeps all client communication organised, searchable, and connected to your business — no emails or external tools required.
