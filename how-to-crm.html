CRM How-To Guide

The CRM in CORE by fibodo helps you manage client relationships, view booking history, communicate with clients, and keep all customer data organised in one place.

1. Open the CRM

Where to go:
Use the left-hand menu and click CRM.

What you’ll see:

  • A searchable list of all clients
  • Client details such as name, user ID, phone, email, and joining date
  • Action buttons including Invite Client and Add Client With CSV

2. View an individual client profile

Click on a client’s name to open their individual profile.

Inside the client profile you can see:

  • Client name and unique ID
  • Joining date
  • Notes and associated member profiles
  • Booking history
  • Messages related to that client
  • A financial snapshot (where applicable)

3. Edit client details

To update a client’s information:

  1. Open the client profile.
  2. Click Edit Profile.
  3. Update the client’s details, including:
    • First name
    • Last name
    • Email address
    • Phone number
    • Internal notes (up to 200 characters)
  4. Click Update to save changes.

Tip: Notes are internal and useful for tracking preferences, health considerations, or important client context.

4. Invite a new client

You can invite clients individually so they can create their own account.

How it works:

  • Click Invite Client from the CRM screen
  • Enter the client’s email address
  • The client receives an invitation to join and book

This is ideal for onboarding new clients digitally with minimal admin.

5. Add clients in bulk using CSV

To upload multiple clients at once:

  1. Click Add Client With CSV.
  2. Choose your CSV file.
  3. Ensure the file meets the required format.
  4. Click Upload.

CSV requirements:

  • File must be a .csv
  • Headers must be exactly: first_name,last_name,email,mobile_number,notes,send_invitation_email
  • Any missing or extra columns will be rejected

Tip: Use the Download sample CSV link to avoid formatting issues.

6. Search, filter, and manage your client list

The CRM makes it easy to find and manage clients as your list grows.

You can:

  • Search clients by name or email
  • Sort by joining date or membership status
  • Filter clients using built-in filters
  • Download client lists where required

7. View booking history and messages

Within each client profile, you can switch between tabs to see:

  • Booking History – past and upcoming bookings
  • Messages – communications related to the client

This gives you a full picture of each client’s relationship with your business.

8. CRM best practice

  • Keep client details up to date to avoid booking or communication issues
  • Use notes to record important information or preferences
  • Bulk upload clients when migrating from another system
  • Use booking history to personalise client interactions

Result:
Your CRM becomes a single source of truth for client data, bookings, and communication — reducing admin and improving client experience.
