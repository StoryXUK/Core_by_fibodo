Financials How-To Guide

The Financials section in CORE by fibodo gives you a clear view of all transactions across your business. Use it to review sales, refunds, transfers, and download statements for reporting, accounting, or reconciliation.

1. Open Financials

Where to go:
Use the left-hand menu and click Financials.

What you’ll see:

  • A tabbed view including Statements, Transfers, and Itemised
  • A searchable list of all financial transactions
  • Download and date-range filtering options

2. View transaction statements

The Statements tab shows all transaction activity across your business.

This includes:

  • Activity bookings
  • Membership bookings
  • Voucher purchases
  • Sales and refunds

Each row represents a single transaction recorded in real time.

3. Understand the statement columns

Each transaction line displays key information to help with reconciliation.

Common columns include:

  • Transaction Date – when the transaction occurred
  • Transaction Reference – unique reference for tracking
  • Entity Type – booking, membership, or voucher
  • Transaction Type – sale or refund
  • Payment Method – card, cash, wallet, etc.
  • Client Name – who made the payment
  • Event Type – class, course, 1-2-1, or other activity

4. Search for a specific transaction

Use the search bar to quickly locate a transaction.

  1. Click into the Search field.
  2. Enter a client name, transaction reference, or keyword.
  3. The list updates instantly to match your search.

This is useful when responding to client queries or checking individual payments.

5. Filter by date range

You can filter transactions by time period.

  1. Click the date selector (e.g. Current month).
  2. Choose the period you want to review.
  3. The statements list updates automatically.

This helps with weekly, monthly, or custom financial reviews.

6. Download your transactions

All financial data can be downloaded for external use.

  1. Apply any search or date filters you need.
  2. Click Download.
  3. Save the file for accounting, reporting, or record keeping.

Use downloads for:

  • Accountant or bookkeeper reporting
  • Tax submissions
  • Internal financial reviews
  • Audit and reconciliation

7. View transfers and itemised data

Use the additional tabs for deeper financial insight.

  • Transfers – view payouts and movement of funds
  • Itemised – break transactions down into individual components

These views are especially useful for detailed reconciliation and financial analysis.

8. Financials best practice

  • Check Financials regularly to stay on top of cash flow
  • Use filters before downloading to reduce manual work
  • Download statements monthly for accounting records
  • Use transaction references when handling refunds or client queries

Result:
The Financials section gives you a complete, downloadable record of every transaction in one place — making it easy to manage, report, and scale your business with confidence.
