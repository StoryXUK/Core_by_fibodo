How To Guide

Memberships

Build loyalty and recurring revenue with flexible membership tiers.

1. Open Memberships

Where to go:
From the left-hand menu, click Memberships.

What you'll see:

  • Your existing membership tiers
  • Status and billing information
  • A button to create a new membership

2. Create a new membership

To build a new membership tier:

  1. Click Create New Membership.
  2. The membership setup flow will guide you step-by-step.
  3. You can move back and forth between steps before publishing.

3. Step 1 – Basic information

Start by defining the identity of your membership.

In this step you set:

  • Membership name
  • Description (what members get and who it's for)
  • Visibility and availability

This information is shown to clients when they view or purchase the membership.

4. Step 2 – Cost & billing

Next, configure how and when members are charged.

Billing options include:

  • Weekly, monthly, or annual billing cycles
  • Prorated billing for mid-cycle joins, upgrades, or downgrades
  • Optional free trials or introductory periods

Good to know:
Fibodo automatically calculates charges, generates invoices, and issues receipts.

5. Step 3 – Benefits

This is where you define what members can access.

You can include:

  • Classes (limited or unlimited)
  • Courses
  • Events
  • 1:1 sessions
  • Digital or on-demand content

Each benefit can have usage limits or rolling allowances per billing period.

6. Step 4 – Terms & conditions

Set clear rules for how the membership behaves.

Options include:

  • Grace periods after expiry
  • Cancellation windows before renewal
  • Whether memberships are transferable

Clear terms help prevent misunderstandings and protect both you and your members.

7. Free trials and intro periods

You can offer trials on any membership tier.

Trial options include:

  • Fixed number of days
  • Limited number of sessions
  • Restricted access to specific content

Clients are automatically notified before the trial ends.

8. Pausing or freezing memberships

Members can pause their membership when needed.

You control:

  • Maximum pause duration
  • How often pauses are allowed
  • Automatic restart dates

This flexibility improves retention while keeping billing predictable.

9. Restricting content to members only

You can lock products and content behind membership access.

Restrictions can be based on:

  • Membership tier level
  • Active or expired status
  • Specific expiry dates

Non-members are prompted to join when they attempt to access restricted content.

10. Failed renewals and dunning

Fibodo automatically manages failed payments.

Dunning tools include:

  • Email or SMS payment reminders
  • Configurable grace periods
  • Automatic suspension or cancellation after repeated failures

All retries and updates are logged for reporting and accounting.

11. Migrating existing members

You can move members from another system into Fibodo.

Supported migration options:

  • CSV import
  • API-based migration

Imported data can include:

  • Membership tiers
  • Start and end dates
  • Membership history
  • Billing details (securely tokenised)

12. Membership best practice

  • Offer clear, simple tiers to reduce confusion
  • Bundle high-value services to increase retention
  • Use trials to reduce friction for new members
  • Review usage and engagement regularly

Result:
Memberships give you predictable revenue, stronger client relationships, and a flexible way to package your services — all managed from one place.
